Most people assume learning to code requires a CS degree, a bootcamp, or at least someone to guide you. I had none of those.

What I had was a problem I desperately wanted to solve — and the stubbornness to not stop until I could build it myself.

## The Real Starting Point

The mistake most beginners make is trying to learn *everything* before building *anything*. I made that mistake for months. Tutorials without projects are just trivia.

The moment things clicked for me was when I stopped following tutorials and started asking: **what do I need to know to build *this specific thing*?**

That question changes everything.

## What Actually Accelerates Learning

1. **Build something broken, then fix it** — reading documentation is 10x more effective when you're debugging a real error
2. **Constraint is a feature** — pick one stack and go deep before going wide
3. **Talk to the code** — explain what you're building out loud; you'll immediately find the gaps in your understanding

## Where I Am Now

Full-stack developer. Building internal systems, personal tools, and increasingly — AI agents.

The gap between "I don't know how to code" and "I'm building AI agents" is shorter than people think. The path isn't linear, but it's absolutely navigable.

If you're at the beginning: the only mistake is not starting.
